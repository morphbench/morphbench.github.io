TL;DR: MorphBench is a manipulation benchmark spanning 190 modular arm morphologies, with a browser tool that lets you build an arm from tube modules and render it live in 3D — no install.

Most manipulation policies are tied to one fixed robot body. MorphBench makes the body a controlled variable: you compose an arm from parametric tube modules — straight, 30°, 45°, and offset bends — across 4-, 5-, and 6-joint designs, for 190 morphologies in total. On this page you can wire up a morphology in the node-graph editor, hit Generate to build its MJCF model, and drive each joint with live sliders in a three.js viewer, all in the browser. The benchmark pairs these bodies with four tasks — reach, push-cube, a dynamic ball-in-cup, and a deformable-object flip — to study policy representations that generalize across body structure rather than assuming a single robot, with the same modules 3D-printed and tracked via OptiTrack for a sim2real path.
